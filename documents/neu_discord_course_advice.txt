--- TYPICAL DISCORD COURSE ADVICE THEMES ---

Channel: #khoury-courses / #cs-advice

Q: "Which section of CS 3500 should I register for?"
Common responses:
- Take Vido (Vidoje Mihajlovikj) if available — best exam reviews, very clear explanations
- Avoid sections where the professor is new or not well-reviewed on RMP
- Check RateMyCourses and RMP before finalizing your schedule

Q: "Is CS 3000 (Algorithms) hard? What should I know going in?"
Common responses:
- Very math-heavy — review graph theory and dynamic programming before the semester
- The homework is time-consuming; start early
- Professor matters a lot; some professors give more structured help than others

Q: "Should I take Fundies 1 (CS 2500) even if I already know Python?"
Common responses:
- Yes — Fundies uses Racket (a functional language) and teaches a completely different way of thinking
- Don't skip it even if you're an experienced programmer; the design recipe and functional paradigm are tested in later courses
- CS 2500 and CS 2510 are prerequisites for most upper-level CS courses

Q: "What's the fastest path to a SWE co-op?"
Common responses:
- Complete CS 2500, CS 2510, CS 3500, CS 3000 as early as possible
- Build projects on GitHub to supplement coursework
- The co-op office does mock interviews; use those resources

Q: "Is Karl Lieberherr's course worth taking?"
Common responses:
- Mixed reviews — some students find his material interesting but delivery confusing
- Check RMP before registering: ~2.4 quality rating, ~27% would take again, difficulty ~4/5
- If you need his specific course for a requirement, prepare for a challenging semester

Q: "Is CS 3650 (Computer Systems) as hard as people say?"
Common responses:
- Yes — it's one of the hardest required courses. Heavy C programming and low-level memory management.
- Nathaniel Tuck is knowledgeable but demanding. Gene Cooperman has very helpful office hours.
- Start the projects immediately — they take 20+ hours each and are not possible to rush.
- The course is worth it for systems, embedded, and security co-ops. Very respected on a resume.

Q: "How do I prepare for the Fundies 1 design recipe?"
Common responses:
- Read "How to Design Programs" (HtDP) before the semester starts — it's free online.
- The design recipe is non-negotiable. Professors will literally take off points if you skip steps even if your code runs correctly.
- Racket syntax is strange at first but you get used to it within two weeks.

Q: "What's the workload difference between CS 3500 sections?"
Common responses:
- All sections use the same assignment set, so workload is similar regardless of professor.
- The difference is in lecture quality and exam difficulty. Vido's exam reviews are the best.
- Clark Freifeld is organized and helpful during office hours.
- Benjamin Lerner's section is the most challenging but also the most thorough.

Q: "Can I skip CS 2500 if I already know Java?"
Common responses:
- No — the department will not let you skip it without a waiver, and waivers are rarely granted.
- Even if you know Java, Fundies teaches a completely different programming paradigm (functional, bottom-up design).
- Students who skip it via waiver often struggle in CS 2510 and CS 3500 because they missed the design recipe foundation.

Q: "What do students think about Matthias Felleisen?"
Common responses:
- He's a legend in the programming languages world — co-created Racket and wrote HtDP.
- His classes are challenging (4.4/5 difficulty on RMP) but his grading is more lenient than you'd expect (85% = A).
- Some students love his engaging lecture style; others find him disorganized or high-ego.
- Worth taking at least one course with him for the perspective, especially if you're interested in PL or compilers.

Q: "How bad is Karl Lieberherr's course really?"
Common responses:
- Genuinely one of the most complained-about courses in Khoury. 2.4/5 on RMP, 27% would take again.
- Assignments take 6–13 hours per week. Material is interesting (Law of Demeter, aspect-oriented programming) but execution is poor.
- Only take it if you have no other option or you're deeply interested in his specific research area.
- Several students report that the course materials feel AI-generated with little quality control.
